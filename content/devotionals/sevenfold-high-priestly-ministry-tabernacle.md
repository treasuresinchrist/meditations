---
title: Christ's Sevenfold High Priestly Ministry in the Tabernacle
---

The Tabernacle, in its structure, furniture, and order of approach, has everything pointing to the Person and Work of our Lord Jesus Christ.

Each step draws us nearer, from the outer court to the Most Holy Place, revealing how Christ brings sinners into communion with God.

Through it, we behold the sevenfold ministry of our Great High Priest, the Lord Jesus Christ.

---

# 1. Ministry of Introduction at the Gate

> “And for the gate of the court shall be an hanging of blue, and purple, and scarlet...”  
> — Exodus 27:16

> “I am the door: by me if any man enter in, he shall be saved...”  
> — John 10:9

### Christ is the only way of access and acceptance

The gate was the single entrance into the court, reminding us that there is only one way to God.

In Christ alone we find forgiveness, fellowship, and the right to enter God’s presence.

---

# 2. Ministry of Reconciliation at the Brazen Altar

> “...it is a burnt offering unto the LORD: it is a sweet savour...”  
> — Exodus 29:18

> “...he appeared to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself.”  
> — Hebrews 9:26

### Christ is the sacrifice who brings us back to God

The altar was the first object one met upon entering the court, declaring that no one approaches God without shed blood.

Christ offered Himself once for all, reconciling us to the Father through His perfect sacrifice.

---

# 3. Ministry of Separation at the Bronze Laver

> “Thou shalt also make a laver of brass... to wash withal.”  
> — Exodus 30:18

> “Sanctify them through thy truth: thy word is truth.”  
> — John 17:17

### Christ cleanses and sanctifies His own

After the altar came the laver, where the priests washed before serving.

Those redeemed by blood must also be washed for holiness.

Christ not only saves His people but continues daily to sanctify them through His Word.

---

# 4. Ministry of Illumination at the Lampstand

> “...they shall light the lamps... that they may give light...”  
> — Exodus 25:37

> “I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in darkness, but shall have the light of life.”  
> — John 8:12

### Christ is the Light who guides and enables true worship

In the Holy Place, there was no natural light; only the lampstand illumined it.

Apart from Christ, there is no wisdom, no spiritual sight, and no fellowship with God.

---

# 5. Ministry of Satisfaction at the Table of Shewbread

> “...thou shalt set upon the table shewbread before me alway.”  
> — Exodus 25:30

> “I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger...”  
> — John 6:35

### Christ is the Bread who sustains and satisfies

The twelve freshly renewed loaves spoke of God’s continual provision.

In Christ we find nourishment, strength, and fullness for every need of the soul.

---

# 6. Ministry of Intercession at the Altar of Incense

> “And Aaron shall burn thereon sweet incense every morning... a perpetual incense before the LORD...”  
> — Exodus 30:7–8

> “...he ever liveth to make intercession for them.”  
> — Hebrews 7:25

### Christ ever lives to intercede for His people

The rising incense shadowed prayer continually ascending to God.

Our High Priest prays unceasingly, perfectly presenting our needs before the Father.

---

# 7. Ministry of Communion at the Ark of the Covenant

> “And there I will meet with thee, and I will commune with thee...”  
> — Exodus 25:22

> “Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus...”  
> — Hebrews 10:19

### Christ is our boldness to approach the throne of grace

At the mercy seat God met directly with His people.

Through Christ’s blood we are welcomed into the very presence of God, to commune with Him freely and confidently.

---

# The Tabernacle Journey — Christ - The Way, The Truth, and The Life

The journey through the Tabernacle beautifully unfolds the words of our Lord:

> “I am the way, the truth, and the life.”  
> — John 14:6

---

## In the Court — Christ is The Way

Christ is the Gate by which we enter, the Altar where we are reconciled to God, and the Laver where we are cleansed and sanctified.

He brings sinners from the outside world into a place of acceptance, forgiveness, and holiness.

---

## In the Holy Place — Christ is The Truth

Christ is the Lampstand who enlightens, the Table who satisfies, and the Incense Altar through which prayer and fellowship rise.

Here He guides, nourishes, and upholds His people in their daily walk.

---

## In the Most Holy Place — Christ is The Life

Christ is the Ark where God meets with man through His blood.

Here there is no fear of death or judgment, but boldness, communion, and life in the very presence of God.

---

Every step toward God is found in Christ alone.

**He is our Way, our Truth, and our Life.**
