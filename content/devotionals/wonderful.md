---
title: His Name Shall Be Called Wonderful
---

> **"For unto us a child is born, unto us a son is given: and the government shall be upon his shoulder: and his name shall be called Wonderful."**
>
> — Isaiah 9:6

Wonderful is the name given by prophecy, and Wonderful is the Person who fulfilled that prophecy.

Indeed, Wonderful is our Saviour, the Lord Jesus Christ, and Wonderful is His accomplished work on the Cross. From the manger to the empty tomb and His ascension, Christ was—and is—gloriously, eternally Wonderful.

## 1. His Birth was Wonderful

> **"And all they that heard it wondered at those things which were told them by the shepherds."**
>
> — Luke 2:18

The shepherds spoke of a Saviour who is Christ the Lord, and the people wondered. The Creator lying in a manger, the Infinite becoming an infant, was a wonder. His Birth was indeed Wonderful.

---

## 2. His Wisdom was Wonderful

> **"And all that heard him were astonished at his understanding and answers."**
>
> — Luke 2:47

At the age of twelve, the teachers were overwhelmed, for He spoke as a Master revealing divine truths. Indeed, the Word became flesh, and the words spoken by Him were Wonderful.

---

## 3. His Power was Wonderful

> **"But the men marvelled, saying, What manner of man is this, that even the winds and the sea obey him!"**
>
> — Matthew 8:27

The winds and the sea obeyed Him. Creation recognised its Creator's voice. The disciples were not merely relieved—they marvelled. No prophet had ever spoken to the winds. No priest had ever silenced the sea. His Power was indeed Wonderful.

---

## 4. His Death was Wonderful

> **"And Pilate marvelled if he were already dead: and calling unto him the centurion, he asked him whether he had been any while dead."**
>
> — Mark 15:44

Pilate, hardened by countless executions, marvelled at the Lord's death. The Lord laid down His own life—voluntarily, majestically, and victoriously. The Cross was not an accident; it was an accomplishment, and even Pilate knew that His death was unlike any other. His Death was indeed Wonderful.

---

## 5. His Resurrection was Wonderful

> **"Then arose Peter, and ran unto the sepulchre; and stooping down, he beheld the linen clothes laid by themselves, and departed, wondering in himself at that which was come to pass."**
>
> — Luke 24:12

Peter came trembling but departed wondering. The stone rolled away, the grave clothes undisturbed, and the empty tomb all testified to His victory. Indeed, Wonderful was His Resurrection—a resurrection that turned sorrow into song, fear into faith, and despair into delight.

---

Indeed, our Lord Jesus Christ is Wonderful.

- **His Birth** — Heaven touching earth.
- **His Wisdom** — The Father revealed through the Son.
- **His Power** — Creation obeying its Creator.
- **His Death** — Love suffering in majesty.
- **His Resurrection** — Life triumphing forever.

He is Wonderful in His Person.  
He is Wonderful in His wisdom.   
He is Wonderful in His ways.  
He is Wonderful in His work.  
He is Wonderful in His worth.
