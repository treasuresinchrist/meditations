---
title: The Three Arks
---

Throughout Scripture, the ark appears in different settings, each revealing a precious aspect of the Person and work of the Lord Jesus Christ.

The three arks unfold:

- **His Person**
- **His Work**
- **His Glory**

---

# 1. The Ark of Bulrushes — Christ in His Humility

> “And when she could not longer hide him, she took for him an ark of bulrushes, and daubed it with slime and with pitch, and put the child therein; and she laid it in the flags by the river’s brink.”  
> — Exodus 2:3

> “By faith Moses, when he was born, was hid three months of his parents...”  
> — Hebrews 11:23

Moses was placed in an ark during a time of death and oppression. God preserved a beautiful child through whom deliverance would come.

This points to Christ in His incarnation — the tender plant, born under the law, entering into our danger and sorrows, yet perfectly pleasing to God.

> “For he shall grow up before him as a tender plant, and as a root out of a dry ground.”  
> — Isaiah 53:2

> “I am meek and lowly in heart.”  
> — Matthew 11:29

Christ came down in humility to redeem His people.

The Ark of Bulrushes speaks of **Christ’s Person** — the Son of God who humbled Himself and entered this world of suffering.

---

# 2. The Ark of Noah — Christ Bearing Judgment and Bringing Salvation

> Genesis 6–8  
> Hebrews 11:7

The ark saved Noah’s house from judgment.

Inside the ark was life; outside was death.

This speaks of Christ in His death and resurrection — the place of safety from judgment.

At the Cross, judgment fell upon Him. In Christ, a new life began.

> “There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus.”  
> — Romans 8:1

Christ bore judgment and became the Head of a new creation, a people who are entirely free from condemnation and who shall be to the eternal pleasure and glory of God.

The Ark of Noah speaks of **Christ’s Work** — His finished work of redemption, where judgment was borne and salvation was secured.

---

# 3. The Ark of the Covenant — Christ Glorified Before God

> “And after the second veil, the tabernacle which is called the Holiest of all; which had the golden censer, and the ark of the covenant overlaid round about with gold...”  
> — Hebrews 9:3–5

The ark in the Most Holy Place speaks of Christ glorified and abiding forever before God.

Inside the ark were:

- The **golden pot of manna** — speaking of His perfect life treasured up before God.
- **Aaron’s rod that budded** — speaking of His resurrection life and eternal priesthood.
- **The tables of the law** — speaking of God’s will perfectly fulfilled in Him.

Above the ark, the **cherubim of glory** speaks of God’s holiness and glory upheld, while the mercy seat speaks of how, in Christ, all God’s claims are satisfied and grace reigns.

> “We have such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens.”  
> — Hebrews 8:1

In Christ, we have a Great High Priest set in the heavens for us.

The Ark of the Covenant speaks of **Christ’s Glory** — the exalted Son of God, dwelling before God and representing His people forever.

---

# Summary

The three arks reveal three glorious aspects of the Lord Jesus Christ:

| Ark | Speaks of | Revelation of Christ |
|---|---|---|
| Ark of Bulrushes | His Humility | Christ came down for us |
| Ark of Noah | His Work | Christ bore judgment and brought salvation |
| Ark of the Covenant | His Glory | Christ is exalted forever before God |

Together, they unfold:

**His Person — His Work — His Glory**

The One who came down in humility is the One who bore our judgment, and He is now exalted forever in the presence of God as our Great High Priest.
