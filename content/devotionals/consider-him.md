---
title: "Consider Him"
date: 2026-07-13
---

## 1. Consider Him

> For consider him that endured such contradiction of sinners against himself, lest ye be wearied and faint in your minds.  
> **Hebrews 12:3**

The Lord Jesus endured the contradiction of sinners — rejection, hostility, and the cross itself. Yet He did not turn away. The One who suffered so much remained perfectly obedient to the Father.

---

## 2. Consider the Apostle and High Priest

> Wherefore, holy brethren, partakers of the heavenly calling, consider the Apostle and High Priest of our profession, Christ Jesus.  
> **Hebrews 3:1**

He was sent from God as the Apostle, and now represents His people before God as the High Priest.

---

## 3. Consider how great this man

> Now consider how great this man was, unto whom even the patriarch Abraham gave the tenth of the spoils.  
> **Hebrews 7:4**

Melchizedek points to a Greater and Holy High Priest — the Lord Jesus, whose priesthood endures forever.

---

## 4. Consider what I say

> Consider what I say; and the Lord give thee understanding in all things.  
> **2 Timothy 2:7**

The Lord, through His Word, gives us understanding of all things.

---

## 5. Consider the ravens

> Consider the ravens: for they neither sow nor reap; which neither have storehouse nor barn; and God feedeth them.  
> **Luke 12:24**

Even the ravens quietly testify to the faithful provision of God.

---

## 6. Consider the lilies

> Consider the lilies of the field, how they grow; they toil not, neither do they spin.  
> **Matthew 6:28**

The lilies stand clothed with beauty by the hand of their Creator.

---

## 7. Consider one another

> And let us consider one another to provoke unto love and to good works.  
> **Hebrews 10:24**

We are to consider one another — thoughtfully encouraging love and good works unto the glory of God.

---

From **the Cross He endured**, to **the Priesthood He holds**, to **the Word He speaks**, to **the care He shows**, and **to His people** — all quietly call us to pause and consider Him, **our Lord Jesus Christ**.

And as we consider Him, we are strengthened — to live lives worthy of His name for His glory.
