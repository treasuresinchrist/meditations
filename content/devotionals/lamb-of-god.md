When people hear the word **"lamb,"** they think gentle, soft, peaceful.

But for an Israelite, a lamb meant **sacrifice**.

A lamb was brought to the altar as a substitute. It was laid down to bleed so that someone else could live. It represented innocence given in the place of guilt.

So when John the Baptist declared,

> "Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world."  
> **John 1:29**

he wasn’t giving Jesus a poetic nickname. He was announcing an atoning sacrifice.

---

## The Lamb and Redemption

In Israel’s history, the lamb stood in the place of the guilty — at Passover, in daily offerings, and in covenant worship.

A lamb meant death, but not meaningless death. It meant **substitution**. It meant **redemption through blood**.

Redemption was never sentimental. It was judicial. It required payment.

Scripture says:

> "And almost all things are by the law purged with blood; and without shedding of blood is no remission."  
> **Hebrews 9:22**

---

## The Lamb Who Came to Fulfill the Picture

The Lord Jesus did not merely receive the title **"Lamb."** He entered history to fulfill it.

He went to the Cross willingly, in perfect unity with the Father's will. The Cross was not an accident. It was justice satisfied and love expressed at the same time.

The innocent for the guilty.  
The righteous for the unrighteous.  
The sinless for the sinful.

It cost the Son everything — and He laid down His life voluntarily.

> "Therefore doth my Father love me, because I lay down my life, that I might take it again. No man taketh it from me, but I lay it down of myself."  
> **John 10:17-18**

---

## Salvation Purchased

Today we speak easily of forgiveness, but forgiveness demanded a death.

We celebrate mercy, but mercy was purchased.

The Lamb of God is not just a comforting image. It is a holy reminder that our salvation was costly beyond measure.

When we truly understand what the Lamb means, salvation stops being a slogan and becomes a divine reality.

Gratitude replaces mere words and becomes worship.

Worship that bows.  
Worship that obeys.  
Worship that surrenders.

---

## The Worthy Lamb

You are forgiven today because the Lamb was led to slaughter — and He did not turn away.

He stood in our place.  
He bore our sins.  
He endured what we deserved.  
And He finished it.

> "Worthy is the Lamb that was slain, and hath redeemed us to God by thy blood..."  
> **Revelation 5:12, 5:9**

The Lamb was slain — and heaven calls Him worthy.

Indeed, the Lamb of God is worthy not only of our thanks, but also of our whole lives.
