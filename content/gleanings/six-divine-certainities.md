---
title: Six Divine Certainties
---
One of the greatest blessings in a believer's life is to have the heart established in the certainty of God's Word. Throughout Scripture, God has not left His people to uncertainty or speculation. Again and again, He gives us truths that are firm, unchanging, and worthy of complete confidence.

Our faith does not rest upon human opinion or changing circumstances, but upon the unfailing Word of God. What He has spoken stands sure, and every promise, every purpose, and every declaration of God will be fulfilled.

The Scriptures therefore encourage us to continue in the things we have learned and have been assured of, holding fast the faith once delivered unto the saints. As we walk through an ever-changing world, it is a great comfort to know that there are divine certainties which remain unshaken.

Let us consider six things in Scripture that are described as **"sure."**

---

## 1. A Sure Foundation

> **"Nevertheless the foundation of God standeth sure..."**<br>
> — 2 Timothy 2:19
 
Everything around us may change, but the foundation that God has laid can never be moved. The believer's confidence does not rest upon human wisdom, religious systems, or changing opinions, but upon the finished work of Christ and the unchanging truth of God. Because the foundation stands sure, our faith stands secure.

---

## 2. A Sure Dwelling Place

> **"And my people shall dwell in a peaceable habitation, and in sure dwellings, and in quiet resting places."**<br>
> — Isaiah 32:18

The world is filled with unrest, fear, and uncertainty. Yet God promises His people a place of peace and security. Even amidst outward trials, the believer enjoys the quiet confidence that comes from dwelling under God's care and protection.

---

## 3. A Sure Hope

> **"Which hope we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and steadfast..."**<br>
> — Hebrews 6:19

The believer's hope is not mere wishful thinking but a certainty anchored in heaven itself. Christ has entered within the veil as our Forerunner, guaranteeing all that God has promised. Our hope is therefore sure, steadfast, and unmovable because it rests upon Him.

---

## 4. A Sure Word


> **"We have also a more sure word of prophecy..."**<br>
> — 2 Peter 1:19

In a world filled with changing voices and conflicting opinions, God has given us a sure Word. The Scriptures remain our unfailing guide, shining as a light in a dark place until the day dawns and the Day Star arises in our hearts. Blessed is the believer who takes heed to God's Word.

---

## 5. A Sure Judgment

> **"We are sure that the judgment of God is according to truth..."**<br>
> — Romans 2:2–6
 
God's judgment is never arbitrary, partial, or mistaken. He judges according to truth and will render to every man according to his deeds. The unbeliever will stand before the Great White Throne, while believers will appear before the Judgment Seat of Christ to give account of their service. The certainty of coming judgment should lead us to holy living and faithful service.

---

## 6. A Sure Reward

> **"To him that soweth righteousness shall be a sure reward."**
> — Proverbs 11:18
> 
No labor performed for the Lord is ever forgotten. Every act of faithfulness, however small or unnoticed by men, is known to Him. The believer serves not for earthly recognition but in the confidence that the Lord Himself will reward every work done for His glory.

---

God has not left His people to uncertainty. He has given us:

- A Sure Foundation on which to stand.
- A Sure Dwelling Place in which to rest.
- A Sure Hope that anchors the soul.
- A Sure Word to guide our path.
- A Sure Judgment that reminds us of our accountability.
- A Sure Reward that encourages faithful service.

These divine certainties remain unchanged because they rest upon the unchanging character and unfailing Word of God.
