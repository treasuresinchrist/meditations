---
title: Four Things Crucified at the Cross
---

The Cross of Christ not only accomplished our redemption before God, but
it also has a profound practical effect upon the believer.
Scripture
speaks of four things that are crucified in connection with the death of
Christ.

## 1. The Old Man Crucified

> **"Knowing this, that our old man is crucified with him..."**  
> — Romans 6:6

The expression **"crucified with"** means to be crucified in company
with another, just as the two thieves were crucified with the Lord
Jesus. The **old man** which is our former life in Adam-the whole character
of the unregenerate person is now crucified.

When the Gibeonites came to Joshua, they carried **old sacks, old wine
bottles, old garments, and old shoes** (Joshua 9). Everything about them
spoke of what was old and worn out. Joshua was deceived into accepting
what should have been rejected.

Our old habits formed in sin should no longer dominate us when we see them at Calvary.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Self Crucified

> **"I am crucified with Christ."**  
> — Galatians 2:20

This is not **self-crucifixion**, but **crucifixion with Christ**.

A dead leaf remains attached to the tree for a time, but an unseen
barrier forms within the stalk. The life has already been cut off. Soon
the wind carries the leaf away.

So it is with the believer. The Cross has placed a barrier between us
and the life of self. We are not called to put ourselves to death by our
own efforts, but to believe God's declaration that we have died with
Christ. As faith lays hold of this truth, self loses its control over
us.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. The Flesh Crucified

> **"They that are Christ's have crucified the flesh with the affections
> and lusts."**  
> — Galatians 5:24

The **flesh** is the sinful principle that remains in every believer. It
cannot be improved or educated; it is incurably opposed to God.

Because we belong to Christ, we are to treat the flesh as a condemned
thing. By faith, we bring the Cross of Christ into every temptation,
refusing to allow the desires of the flesh to rule our lives.

The flesh was never meant to become stronger---it is to remain under the
sentence of the Cross.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. The World Crucified

> **"By whom the world is crucified unto me, and I unto the world."**  
> — Galatians 6:14

The **world** is man's organized system apart from God---its religion,
wisdom, pleasures, ambitions, and glory.

If we want to know what the world truly is, we only need to look at what it
did to the Son of God. The world crucified Christ.

Scripture describes:

-   The wisdom of the world (1 Corinthians 1:21)
-   This present evil world (Galatians 1:4)
-   The course of this world (Ephesians 2:2)
-   The rulers of the darkness of this world (Ephesians 6:12)
-   The friendship of the world (James 4:4)
-   The pollutions of the world (2 Peter 2:20)
-   The things of the world (1 John 2:15--17)

To the believer, the world is a crucified thing, and the believer is
crucified to the world. Its attractions have lost their rightful claim
upon the heart that has seen Christ upon the Cross.

------------------------------------------------------------------------


The Cross of Christ speaks of four great crucifixions in relation to the believer:

1.  **The Old Man Crucified** - Romans 6:6.
2.  **Self Crucified** - Galatians 2:20.
3.  **The Flesh Crucified** - Galatians 5:24.
4.  **The World Crucified** - Galatians 6:14.

The Cross not only saves us from the penalty of sin, but also teaches us
how to live. The old man has been judged, self has been displaced, the
flesh has been condemned, and the world has lost its hold.

As we live in the light of these truths, Christ Himself becomes the center and
satisfaction of our lives.
